# Architecture Development

the process was already described partly in [Correct Procedure](02-correct-procedure.md)


> "The life of a software architect is a long and rapid succession of suboptimal design decisions taken partly in the dark."
>
> — Philippe Kruchten, [The Accidental Architecture](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/1628932/), IEEE Software, 2006


Steps to identify architecture decisions:

1. *Question:* what is the problem? What impact has the decision?
2. *Constraints:* What constraints exists? Which risks are affected? what are quality goals?
3. *Assumptions:* Which assumptions were made? Which assumptions can be verified before?
4. *Checked alternatives:* Which options are in scope? How are they rated?
5. *Decision:* Who made the decision? What is the reasioning? Why was it made?

The rating of decisions should be rated with relevant stakeholders and all stakeholders must be aware of consequrences.
